Q: What is the significance of Bismillah?
A: "Bismillah" (In the name of Allah) marks the beginning of almost every Surah in the Quran. It serves as a reminder to start every action with the name of God, seeking His blessing and support.

Q: What does the Quran say about patience (Sabr)?
A: Typically, the Quran emphasizes patience as a virtue. It is often mentioned alongside prayer and trust in Allah. Believers are encouraged to be patient in times of hardship, promising that Allah is with those who are patient.

Q: Who are the People of the Book?
A: The "People of the Book" (Ahl al-Kitab) refers to Jews and Christians, recipients of earlier divine scriptures (the Torah and the Gospel). The Quran acknowledges their bond with Muslims while highlighting theological differences.

Q: What is the Night of Power (Laylat al-Qadr)?
A: Laylat al-Qadr is considered the holiest night in Islam, found in the last ten odd nights of Ramadan. It is the night when the first verses of the Quran were revealed to Prophet Muhammad. Worship on this night is described as better than a thousand months.

Q: What is the story of Prophet Musa (Moses) and Khidr?
A: This story, found in Surah Al-Kahf, illustrates the limits of human knowledge and the wisdom of Allah's plan. Musa travels with Al-Khidr, who performs seemingly confusing acts (scuttling a ship, killing a boy, rebuilding a wall). Khidr later explains the hidden wisdom behind each act, teaching Musa patience and trust.
